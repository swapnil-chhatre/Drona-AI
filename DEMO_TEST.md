# Year 8 (Class 8 Equivalent) – Australian Curriculum Overview

## English
- Novel study (Australian & global literature)
- Poetry analysis (themes, imagery, tone)
- Persuasive writing (essays, speeches)
- Creative writing (short stories, narratives)
- Media literacy (advertisements, films, digital texts)
- Oral presentations and debates

---

## Mathematics
- Algebra (linear equations, expressions)
- Geometry (angles, triangles, congruence)
- Measurement (area, volume)
- Statistics and probability (graphs, data interpretation)
- Ratios and percentages
- Coordinate geometry (graphing)

---

## Science

### Biology
- Cells and their functions
- Human body systems

### Chemistry
- Elements and compounds
- Chemical reactions (basic)

### Physics
- Forces and motion
- Energy transformations

### Earth Science
- Rock cycle
- Structure of the Earth

### Skills
- Scientific investigations
- Experimentation and reporting

---

## Humanities

### History
- Medieval Europe (feudalism, castles)
- Ancient China / Japan / Islamic civilizations
- Vikings or Mongols (varies by school)

### Geography
- Landforms and landscapes
- Urbanisation
- Environmental sustainability
- Global interconnections

---

## Digital Technologies
- Introduction to coding (Scratch, Python, or JavaScript basics)
- Algorithms and problem-solving
- Data representation
- Cyber safety and digital ethics

---

## The Arts
- Visual Arts (drawing, painting, design)
- Drama (acting, performance)
- Music (instruments, composition)
- Media Arts (film, editing)

---

## Health & Physical Education (PDHPE)
- Physical fitness and sports
- Nutrition and healthy lifestyle
- Mental health and wellbeing
- Relationships and personal development

---

## Design & Technologies
- Product design (woodwork, basic engineering)
- Food technology (cooking skills)
- Sustainability in design

---

## Languages (Optional)
- French / Japanese / Indonesian (varies by school)
- Basic conversation and grammar
- Cultural understanding

---

## Key Learning Approach
- Focus on critical thinking and application
- Emphasis on communication and presentations
- Project-based and collaborative learning
