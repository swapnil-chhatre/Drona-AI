# NSW Year 10 History Study Plan: The Causes of World War I

**Subject:** History  
**Grade:** Year 10  
**Topic:** The Causes of World War I (Focusing on M.A.I.N. and Australian Involvement)  
**Duration:** 4 Lessons (Approx. 60 minutes each)  
**Curriculum Alignment:** NSW Syllabus (HT5-1, HT5-4, HT5-9)

---

## 1. Learning Objectives
By the end of this unit, students will be able to:
*   **Explain** the M.A.I.N. framework (Militarism, Alliances, Imperialism, Nationalism) as the long-term causes of WWI.
*   **Analyze** how the alliance systems (Triple Entente vs. Triple Alliance) created a "domino effect" after the assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand.
*   **Evaluate** Australia’s connection to the British Empire and why this necessitated involvement in the conflict.
*   **Interpret** primary sources from the Australian War Memorial (AWM) to understand the Australian perspective on the outbreak of war.

---

| Lesson | Focus | Key Activity |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |

## 3. Detailed Lesson Plans

### Lesson 1: The M.A.I.N. Framework
*   **Objective:** Define and apply the M.A.I.N. acronym.
*   **Activity:** 
    *   **Direct Instruction:** Introduce M.A.I.N. (Militarism, Alliances, Imperialism, Nationalism).
    *   **Group Work:** Divide the class into four groups. Each group researches one letter of the acronym and creates a "Fact Sheet" explaining how it created tension in Europe by 1914.
*   **Resource:** Teacher-provided `world_war_1.txt`.

### Lesson 2: Alliances & The Spark
*   **Objective:** Understand how local conflict escalated into global war.
*   **Activity:**
    *   **Mapping:** Students draw a map of Europe in 1914, color-coding the Triple Entente (France, Britain, Russia) and the Triple Alliance (Germany, Austria-Hungary, Italy).
    *   **Simulation:** Use a "Domino Effect" activity to trace the diplomatic failures following the assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand.
*   **Resource:** `world_war_1.txt` (Alliance systems).

### Lesson 3: Australia’s Path to War
*   **Objective:** Understand Australia’s position as a young nation within the British Empire.
*   **Activity:**
    *   **Discussion:** Why did Australia join the war? Discuss the concept of "Imperial Loyalty" and the famous quote by Andrew Fisher: *"Australia will stand beside our own to help and defend her to the last man and the last shilling."*
    *   **Primary Source:** Examine AWM archival documents regarding the initial public reaction to the declaration of war in Australia.
*   **Resource:** [Australian War Memorial - First World War](https://www.awm.gov.au/learn/schools/resources/firstworldwar)

### Lesson 4: Synthesis & Assessment
*   **Objective:** Consolidate learning and assess understanding.
*   **Activity:** 
    *   **Source Analysis:** Students select one primary source from the AWM website (e.g., a recruitment poster or a diary entry) and explain how it reflects the causes of the war or the Australian attitude toward it.

---

## 4. Assessment Strategy

### Formative Assessment (Ongoing)
*   **Exit Tickets:** At the end of each lesson, students write one sentence explaining how that day's topic contributed to the outbreak of war.
*   **Map Check:** Review the accuracy of the Alliance mapping in Lesson 2.

### Summative Assessment (Task)
**"The Road to War" Infographic**
*   **Task:** Create an infographic that explains the causes of WWI.
*   **Requirements:**
    1.  Define M.A.I.N. with a specific example for each.
    2.  Include a map showing the two major alliance blocks.
    3.  Write a 200-word paragraph explaining why Australia felt compelled to join the war (referencing the British Empire connection).
    4.  Include one primary source image from the AWM website with a brief analysis of its significance.

---

## 5. Teacher Tips for NSW Context
*   **Differentiation:** Provide a scaffolded template for the M.A.I.N. definitions for students needing support.
*   **Extension:** Ask advanced students to research the "Balkan Powder Keg" and present why that specific region was the catalyst for the alliance systems to trigger.
*   **AWM Integration:** Encourage students to explore the [AWM Encyclopedia](https://www.awm.gov.au/learn/schools/resources/firstworldwar) for their infographic research to ensure they are using high-quality, verified historical data.
